## Step-by-step

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add a post file">
    Drop a <InlineCode>.mdx</InlineCode> file in <InlineCode>content/posts/</InlineCode>. Export a <InlineCode>postMeta</InlineCode> object with title, date, description, and optional tags. The slug comes from the filename — <InlineCode>hello-world.mdx</InlineCode> becomes <InlineCode>/notes/hello-world</InlineCode>.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use locale suffixes (optional)">
    For bilingual posts, use <InlineCode>my-post.en.mdx</InlineCode> and <InlineCode>my-post.zh.mdx</InlineCode> under the same slug. Files without a suffix default to English.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write MDX body">
    Standard markdown works out of the box. Import shortcodes from <InlineCode>mdx-components.tsx</InlineCode> — <InlineCode>Callout</InlineCode>, <InlineCode>CodeBlock</InlineCode>, <InlineCode>Steps</InlineCode>, and more.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preview locally">
    Run <InlineCode>pnpm dev</InlineCode> and visit <InlineCode>/en/notes/your-slug</InlineCode>. No manual registry — <InlineCode>lib/posts.ts</InlineCode> scans the filesystem at build time.
  </Step>
</Steps>
